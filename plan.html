Directions

RESERVATIONS ARE REQUIRED TO DRIVE INTO YOSEMITE. You can drive to Yosemite all year and enter via Highways 41, 140, and 120 from the west. Tioga Pass Entrance (via Highway 120 from the east) is closed from around November through late May or June. Hetch Hetchy is open all year but may close intermittently due to snow. Please note that GPS units do not always provide accurate directions to or within Yosemite.

Operating Hours & Seasons

Due to current health guidelines, if you are driving a vehicle into Yosemite National Park, you must pay the park entrance fee and have a day-use reservation (valid for 7 consecutive days for unlimited entries); Upper Pines Campground or Wawona Horse Camp reservation; Yosemite Valley Lodge, Curry Village, or The Ahwahnee reservation; private lodging or vacation rental reservation in Wawona, Yosemite West, or Foresta; Yosemite wilderness permit; Yosemite Half Dome permit; or a commercial use authorization.

Weather

Yosemite National Park covers nearly 1,200 square miles (3,100 square km) in the Sierra Nevada, with elevations ranging from about 2,000 feet (600 m) to 13,000 ft (4,000 m). Yosemite receives 95% of its precipitation between October and May (and over 75% between November and March). Most of Yosemite is blanketed in snow from about November through May. (Yosemite Valley can be rainy or snowy in any given winter storm).
